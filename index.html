About Us

In Hockey Fights Cancer brings together the hockey community to support friends and family affected by cancer. Each of the 31 NHL Clubs celebrates their own Hockey Fights Cancer Night, each November. Each player wears a special Hockey fights cancer jersey and coaches and broadcasters wear HFC ties. The organization helps raise money and support patients of the American Cancer Society, Canadian Cancer Society, and Movember Foundation.

Inspiring Stories

  • Mighty Quinn
    A 3-year-old bruins fan, being treated for chemotherapy for a brain tumor, was able to walk on the ice at TD garden dropping the ceremonial puck on Hockey Fights Cancer Night.
  • Braden Nienaber
    An eight-year-old boy with Langerhans cell histiocytosis, a disease similar to cancer, spent the weekend In Washington D.C. He got the opportunity to skate with the Capitals.
  • Graysen Hooper
    A 2-year-old who has been fighting leukemia and persevering through chemotherapy for over a year had the opportunity to drop the ceremonial puck with her parents for the Vancouver Canucks and San Jose Sharks.
  • Laila Anderson
    The St. Louis Blues are giving fans the opportunity to sign up for Be a Match registry and receive a swab sample kit to see if you are a potential bone marrow donor for Laila Anderson, a young Blue fan.
